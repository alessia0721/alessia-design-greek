Myths/stories

Greek Mythology holds thousands and thousands
of the most iconic myths and stories ever told. Greek Mythology wouldn't
be the same without these tales.

Here are the top 3 myths/stories:

#2: Story of Cronus.

Cronus was the ruling Titan who came to power
by castrating his Father Uranus.
His wife was Rhea.
There offspring were the first of the Olympians.
To insure his safety Cronus
ate each of the children as they were born.
This worked until Rhea,
unhappy at the loss of her children,
tricked Cronus into swallowing a rock,
instead of Zeus. When he grew up Zeus would revolt
against Cronus and the other Titans,
defeat them, and banish them
to Tartarus in the underworld.

#1: Myth of the fall of Icarus.

Daedalus, a mythical inventor,
created wings made of feathers and wax
to escape from Crete where he and his son,
Icarus, were held captive by King Minos.
Icarus, however, ignored his father's warnings
and flew too close to the sun.
His wings melted and he fell
into the sea where he met his end.

#3: Story of the amazons.

The amazons, were a group of female warriors and hunters,
who surpassed all men in physical agility
and strength, in archery, riding skills,
and the arts of combat.
